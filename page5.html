Adverbs of Frequency

How Often?

Adverbs of frequency tell us how often something happens. They answer the question "How often...?".

Common Adverbs of Frequency

  • always (100%)
  • usually (~80-90%)
  • often (~60-70%)
  • sometimes (~40-50%)
  • seldom (~10-20%)
  • rarely / hardly ever (~5%)
  • never (0%)

Ever is mainly used in questions (Do you ever...?) and negative statements (not ever = never).

Placement Rules

  • Before the main verb:
    Example: I always go running. She sometimes plays soccer. They never eat popcorn.
  • After the verb 'to be' (am, is, are, was, were):
    Example: He is often late. We are usually at home on Sundays.
  • Between an auxiliary verb and the main verb:
    (Auxiliary verbs include: do, does, did, have, has, had, will, can, could, should, etc.)
    Example: Tom and David hardly ever do Taekwondo. (Here 'do' is the main verb).
    Example: She doesn't often go to the movies. I have never seen that film.
  • Beginning of a sentence: Sometimes and Usually can sometimes start a sentence.
    Example: Sometimes he plays soccer.

Asking Questions

  • How often...?
    Example: How often do you take a shower? / How often does Steve play soccer?
  • Do/Does ... ever...?
    Example: Do you ever play tennis? / Does your friend ever do boxing?

Negative Sentences

  • Put don't or doesn't before the adverb and main verb.
    Example: Sara always goes running -> Sara doesn't always go running.
    Example: I sometimes go -> I don't sometimes go. (Or more naturally: I rarely go / I don't go often).
    Example: We never go hiking -> We don't ever go hiking. (Or keep 'never': We never go hiking.)

Other Frequency Expressions

  • You can also use expressions like:
    every day/week/month/year
    once a day/week/month/year
    twice a day/week/month/year
    three times a day/week/month/year
    Example: I take a shower every day. She eats couscous once a week.
